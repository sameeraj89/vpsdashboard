Business context: Lakeshore operates a 1,200-doctor referral network managed by a 15-person BD field team today running on calls, WhatsApp and spreadsheets — with no central tracking or visit history. The contact centre handles 2,300 inbound patient enquiries per day with agents working with zero patient context on screen. CRM is required to close both gaps: structured referral management and a CTI-integrated contact centre.

Factors favouring Microsoft Dynamics
  • VPS Group already on D365 in UAE — shared licensing, group IT governance
  • Purpose-built doctor visit management workflow matches Lakeshore's field team model
  • Lower annual cost (core modules: ~₹20.5L vs ₹24.8L) and defined implementation fee
  • Referral revenue attribution and cold-doctor flagging are built-in, not add-ons
  • 12-week implementation with committed timeline and phase gates
Factors favouring Salesforce
  • Agentforce GenAI for contact centre — AI-native patient query resolution
  • Health Cloud with native care journey and TPA workflows
  • Larger India healthcare partner ecosystem for implementation
  • Tableau provides advanced analytics capability beyond native CRM reporting
  • Offer expires 10 May 2026 — decision required immediately
Decision Note

Both platforms are commercially competitive at ~₹20–25L/yr. The deciding factors are: (1) Group platform alignment — VPS UAE on D365 creates a clear governance and cost advantage for Dynamics; (2) Use-case fit — the Dynamics proposal is scoped specifically around Lakeshore's 1,200-doctor visit management workflow, whereas Salesforce is broader but generic; (3) AI capability — Salesforce's Agentforce is more mature for contact-centre AI today; (4) Urgency — Salesforce offer lapses 10 May 2026. Recommended basis for decision: confirm with VPS Group IT whether D365 group licensing leverage reduces Dynamics cost materially; if yes, Dynamics is the preferred choice. If no group licensing benefit, Salesforce's AI contact-centre capability at comparable cost is compelling — but requires a partner implementation proposal before commitment.
