✅ Momentum Building
January 2026 is the strongest month (₹43.1 Cr, ADR ₹1.66 Cr) — 28% above April (₹33.9 Cr). Strong Jan performance in Medical Oncology (₹335L IP) and Nephrology (₹389L IP) are driving the step-up.
⚠️ Seasonal Dips to Watch
June (₹33.5 Cr) and November (₹35.3 Cr) are consistent soft spots — likely linked to monsoon and festival seasons. November IP was ₹2,506L vs ₹3,109L in January — a 19% drop in a single month.

What's Working
🚀 Medical Oncology — Growth Engine
₹53.2 Cr (14.4% of revenue), 35K OP visits, 4,810 IP discharges. OP revenue (₹18.8 Cr) indicates strong chemotherapy volumes. Jan IP (₹335L) broadly steady — a reliable base. The single biggest department and getting bigger.

Implication: Adding LINACs directly amplifies this revenue base with incremental radiation revenue.
💎 Nephrology — High-Value Anchor
₹45.3 Cr (12.2%), but the real story is ARPP(IP) of ₹3.13 Lakhs — among the highest in the hospital. January IP revenue (₹389L) is 62% above April (₹240L). Transplant and dialysis volumes are scaling.

Implication: Every additional transplant case is a ₹3+ Lakh ticket. BMT expansion (₹35 Cr capex) will compound this further.
📈 Cardiology — Accelerating
Revenue up from ₹132L (Apr) to ₹205L (Jan) — 55% growth within FY26. ARPP(IP) of ₹1.47L is decent. High OP volume of 13.8K visits feeds the IP funnel consistently.

Implication: Solid trajectory — no immediate intervention needed, focus on protecting consultant retention.
⚡ Urology — Quietly Strong
Up 77% from Apr (₹91L) to Jan (₹161L) IP revenue. ARPP(IP) ₹1.63L and ARPOB ₹51K — above hospital average. 812 discharges with good bed-day efficiency.

Implication: Urology is punching well — worth investing in a second senior urologist to sustain momentum.
🌐 International Revenue
9.2% of revenue (₹35 Cr in 10 months). Peak in Jul–Aug likely NRI summer visits. Strong base for the transformation plan's NRI referral target of ₹35 Cr/year by FY29 — which is achievable even without growth from current levels.

Implication: Needs a structured referral program and dedicated international patient services team.
🔺 January — Best Month Ever
₹43.1 Cr in January 2026 at ADR ₹1.66 Cr — highest single month in the dataset. 2,022 IP discharges (also highest). ARPOB Blended at ₹51,456.

Implication: The hospital is at an inflection point — the underlying demand is there, the bottleneck is case mix, not patients.
What's Not Working
❌ Radiation Oncology — Near Zero IP
IP revenue only ₹0.12 Cr on 13 discharges in 10 months. OP is ₹5.68 Cr (4,250 visits) but that's limited by no LINAC — patients are referred out for radiation. This is the single biggest revenue leak in the hospital.

Implication: Every referred-out radiation patient is ₹3–6 Lakh leaving the building. The ₹70 Cr LINAC investment directly plugs this. Priority #1.
❌ GI Surgery — Revenue Declining
IP revenue fell from ₹243L (Apr) to ₹193L (Jan) — a 20% decline within the year. Despite 1,013 discharges, the trend is negative. ARPP(IP) at ₹2.01L is respectable — so this is a volume problem, not a pricing one.

Implication: Likely losing complex GI cases to Aster Medcity or Amrita. Needs investigation into referral leakage and consultant availability.
❌ Head & Neck Oncology — Volatile
IP revenue swung from ₹146L (Sep) to just ₹12L (Oct) — a one-month 92% collapse — then recovering to ₹63L by Jan. High ARPP(IP) ₹2.94L makes this specialty highly sensitive to volume fluctuations. Only 262 discharges in 10 months.

Implication: Too dependent on individual consultants. Building a team of 2–3 head & neck surgeons stabilises revenue and captures unmet demand.
⚠️ Comprehensive Liver Care — Underscaled
Highest ARPP(IP) in the hospital at ₹3.90 Lakhs per patient, but only 225 discharges in 10 months. This is a massive missed opportunity — 1,000 discharges at this ARPP would add ₹39 Cr of IP revenue annually.

Implication: With 150+ cumulative liver transplants already, reputation exists. The constraint is likely surgeon capacity and referral pathways — not demand.
⚠️ Pediatrics — Low-Value, High-Volume
21,002 OP visits (second highest), 1,159 IP discharges — but ARPP(IP) of only ₹36,664 (lowest in the hospital). Revenue ₹6.33 Cr on massive footprint consumes beds, nursing, and consultant time for poor financial return.

Implication: Consider limiting beds for general paediatrics, and repositioning toward paediatric oncology or paediatric cardiac surgery (higher ARPP) as the case mix shifts.
⚠️ Spine Surgery — Underdeveloped
Highest ARPOB in the hospital at ₹93,510/day. ARPP(IP) ₹3.26 Lakhs. But only 115 discharges in 10 months — tiny for a hospital with this size and reputation. Huge gap vs Joint Replacement (583 discharges at ₹3.22L ARPP).

Implication: Spine is an underpenetrated high-value niche. Recruiting 1–2 senior spine surgeons could add ₹15–20 Cr of revenue with limited bed expansion.
