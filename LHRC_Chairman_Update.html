Strategic Note
VPS Qutub — Delhi Precision Medicine Centre
Delhi is where India's healthcare reputation is made — where national payors set empanelment terms, where complex cases from across North India are referred, and where a differentiated quaternary brand can establish itself at scale. The VPS Qutub Precision Medicine Centre is being conceived as a niche, sub-speciality-led facility that receives referrals from multispecialties rather than competing with them. Positioned in the Qutub Institutional Area of Mehrauli, South Delhi — an MPD-2041 designated institutional zone with FAR 3.5 — the site supports a G+6 structure across five basements and a roof, delivering approximately 2,54,800 sq ft of built-up area.
The clinical programme is built around five high-complexity verticals where demand is national, volume is concentrated, and replication is structurally difficult: Haemato-Oncology & BMT/CAR-T, Rare Disease & Immunology, Neuromuscular, Precision Oncology, and Organ-Specific Transplantation. At 106 licensed beds and 6 operating theatres, the facility is intentionally compact — designed for acuity and margin, not volume. Burjeel's established Gulf referral network provides a structural international patient pipeline from day one.
The concept design brief has been issued. The immediate next step is to engage a local Delhi architect to validate the floor plan, scope regulatory submissions to DUAC and MCD, and confirm the approvals pathway under MPD-2041. A first draft of the expansion plan will be shared with the local architect next week.

Strategic Note
VPS Expansion Pipeline — New Concepts Under Evaluation
A comprehensive strategic expansion pipeline is being developed across four geographies, each with a distinct clinical and commercial rationale. Detailed concept briefs, financial models and market assessments are available in the interactive expansion brief linked below.
VPS Kakkanad — SmartCity Kochi ↗
  • Concept: A 150–200-bed multispecialty second campus at SmartCity Kochi that creates a two-hospital system unique to Kerala — VPS Lakeshore becomes the specialist referral hub (transplant, cardiac surgery, CAR-T, neurosurgery) while VPS Kakkanad serves as the community-facing multispecialty centre for the IT corridor's 140k+ workforce and 320k+ residential catchment. Together they form a managed referral loop no competitor in Kochi can replicate.
  • Strategic rationale: The Kakkanad IT corridor currently sends 40% of patients to Aster Medcity and competitors. No premium inpatient hospital exists inside the SmartCity/Infopark cluster today. A second campus intercepts this leakage while freeing VPS Lakeshore beds for higher-acuity complex cases — improving revenue per bed at both sites.
  • Due diligence & next steps: Land and legal DD on both SmartCity parcels (5.85 Ac and 6.17 Ac options); independent financial feasibility study and demand validation; clinical concept brief and bed-mix planning; corporate TPA pipeline conversations with top Infopark employers. Based on feasibility output, will present further options for consideration.
VPS Qutub — Delhi ↗
  • Concept: India's first private-sector integrated cancer, rare disease and transplant centre — a purpose-built 150-bed facility in the Qutub Institutional Area, South Delhi. Every floor, bed and hire is dedicated to complex care: haem-onc, BMT, CAR-T, genomics, rare disease and organ transplant. No equivalent facility exists in North India — Delhi's cancer care is fragmented across general multispecialties, and there is no private-sector rare disease CoE in NCR.
  • Strategic rationale: Delhi is the single highest-leverage brand investment for VPS — it is where India's healthcare reputation is made, where national payors make empanelment decisions, and where complex cases from across North India are referred. A niche, differentiated centre receives referrals from multispecialties rather than competing with them. Burjeel's Gulf network provides a structural international patient pipeline.
  • Due diligence & next steps: Clinical Architect recruitment by 15 May 2026; evaluating between existing proposals and commissioning a dedicated oncology and healthcare-specific primary and secondary market study by a globally reputed firm such as Ipsos or Nielsen — study to close by 30 June; pharma partnerships (Sanofi, Takeda, ImmunoACT) and hospital collaborations — including Sitaram Bhartia (500m away) as a potential JV for multispecialty backup — to be evaluated and advanced to due diligence from May 2026. Architectural and clinical design to close by 30 June.
Jaipur & Kota — Rajasthan ↗

  • Next steps: Market feasibility study and further discussions with Mr Mukesh Sabharwal (Indorama) to determine partnership structure and VPS's role — and take a Go/No-go decision.

Paperless Shift
  • Transitioning all signatures to DocuSign: All contracts, approvals and formal agreements move to DocuSign going forward — eliminating physical routing delays, creating a tamper-evident audit trail, and ensuring every document is timestamped and retrievable.
  • Printing costs: Total printing spend for the last year was approximately ₹1.7 Cr. The paperless shift is expected to reduce this significantly — a target reduction will be set once baseline usage data is reviewed.
Term Life & Group Cover Insurance
  • Term life and group medical cover review initiated — premiums, coverage and provider terms under assessment. The absence of adequate cover to date leaves the hospital with unhedged liability under the Employees' Compensation Act and is a potential finding in the next NABH audit cycle.

Strategic Note
Tirupati Oncology JV with G2 Healthcare — Do Not Proceed
  • Returns below hurdle: PwC-modelled IRR of 16.6–17.2% across both deal structures falls short of the 20% minimum. Breakeven after Year 9; EBITDA below 25% through Year 10.
  • Crowded market: Tirupati already served by SVICCAR (Tata Trusts-backed, subsidised), Helios and Aster Narayanadri. VPS enters with higher cost base, no referral pipeline and no local brand equity.
  • Deal skewed in G2's favour: VPS funds ~73% of new capex (₹111 Cr) yet pays 10% revenue share to G2, with no exit mechanism and no minimum volume guarantees.
  • Better alternatives exist: Deploy capital into Lakeshore's own clinical services expansion (established brand, patient base, platform); or return to G2 only with renegotiated terms — fairer revenue split, volume guarantees, exit rights.
