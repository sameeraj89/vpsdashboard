Note Summary
Cost — ₹2.4 Cr from manufacturer negotiations and ₹20 L distributor savings confirmed immediate. Payroll target ₹7.6 Cr; ₹4.7 Cr identified. Supplier and distributor renegotiation is the next major lever.
Revenue — Rate revision sign-off due 31 March. Drug substitution (₹4.6 Cr projected margin) and three new specialties at business case stage. All levers must close in Q1.
Compliance — 27 of 62 items past due; IT return, GSTR-9 and board minute filings require immediate action.
Brand Uplift — Org chart due 20 March. Website RFP shortlist by 25 March. Process mapping complete; implementation from 17 March.
Cost Rationalisation
Manufacturer negotiations (₹2.4 Cr) and distributor renegotiations (₹20 L) are the confirmed immediate levers. Suppliers and distributors represent the single largest untapped area for further savings — the top 20 by spend account for the majority of the consumables bill and rate contracts on most have not been renegotiated in over two years. Structured discount conversations with these vendors, benchmarked against peer hospital contracts, are the next priority for the procurement team. Payroll rationalisation continues in parallel with an external HR consultant now engaged.

Compliance
Diagnostic complete across 62 items: 27 are past due, 6 require action within days, and 16 internal contradictions exist between tracking sheets — meaning the true renewal status of key licences (AERB, Fire NOC, Pharmacy, BMW) cannot be confirmed with certainty. Most material exposures: FY25 income tax return four months overdue (Sec 271B penalty risk up to ₹1.5 Cr); GSTR-9 annual return 72 days past deadline with daily penalties accruing; board minutes for three meetings unsigned, blocking DIR-12 and MGT-14 filings due in 22 days. A dedicated compliance champion with a 60-day mandate is required.

Other Notes
UNA Strike — Situation and Response
The United Nurses Association (UNA) strike has affected private hospitals across Kerala, including Lakeshore. Emergency and critical services at Lakeshore have remained fully operational throughout. At this point, we are not planning to take any significant further action — the situation is being monitored and management will respond if conditions materially change.

Key Personnel Transitions
A number of key personnel changes are currently underway. On the clinical side, several doctor transitions are in progress as part of the broader department restructuring. In finance, a new appointment is being finalised to strengthen the team's capacity ahead of the FY27 planning cycle. Separately, an external HR consultant has been engaged to lead the payroll rationalisation work — providing independent oversight of the ₹7.6 Cr reduction target and supporting management on the identification and implementation of the remaining savings.
