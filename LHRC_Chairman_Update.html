Fortnight of 16 March 2026
LHRC/BU/2026/001
DRAFT — Figures represent identified potential across all workstreams and are subject to revision. This update will be updated with actuals as implementation progresses.
Note Summary
₹23.2 Cr in cost savings across payroll, vendors and capex has been identified and ₹17.1 Cr in revenue upside through drug substitution and rate revision for all procedures and nursing costs. Compliance tracker has identified certain gaps that are now being fixed, while the org structure and website redesign will close in a month.
Cost — ~₹23.2 Cr total savings identified across three initiatives: payroll ₹4.7 Cr (~130 staff), vendor renegotiation ₹3.8 Cr, and Capex deferrals ₹14.7 Cr (top 6 departments reviewed).
Revenue — ~₹17.1 Cr revenue upside identified: drug substitution ₹4.6 Cr, cash patient rate revision ₹12.5 Cr (1% IP rate increase, nursing charge pass-through, top-50 procedure correction). BD hire and doctor performance review in progress.
Lakeshore 2.0 — Reworked org structure ready end of month. Website go-live end of April; app under consideration. Process and platform mapping underway.
Compliance — 27 of 62 items past due; IT return, GSTR-9 and board minute filings require immediate action. Compliance champion with 60-day mandate required.
1. Cost Rationalisation
Cost rationalisation programme underway across three workstreams — detail in each section below.

Payroll
  • ₹4.7 Cr identified to date covering ~130 staff across 7 departments — Nursing, Pharmacy and Patient Care Services account for the majority.
  • Benchmarking of various metrics including staff/bed and Dispensing time per drug against comparable hospital systems used.
  • Individual department targets being set through March with implementation from April 2026 — this represents a short and intermediate timeline for action.
Capex Management & Reduction
  • FY27 Capex budget reviewed for top 6 departments; ₹14.7 Cr in potential savings identified through item deferrals, rejections and scope reductions. Remaining departments to be reviewed prior to end of March.
  • Top 5 deferred or removed items account for ₹8.2 Cr of the total saving; details available below.

Vendor Management
  • Drug manufacturer negotiations (21 companies, 5%) — ₹1.2 Cr saving; distributor renegotiation (10 distributors, 5%) — ₹2.0 Cr saving. Combined: ₹3.2 Cr.
  • Top 20 consumable suppliers and items (5%) — ₹0.6 Cr further saving. Total across all four categories: ₹3.8 Cr.
  • Most rate contracts not renegotiated in over two years; structured discount conversations are the next priority. Rate contract calendar being finalised from week of 20 March.
2. Revenue Enhancement
  • Drug substitution (brand → generic) across high-volume pharmacy lines; ₹4.6 Cr projected additional margin from formulary shift — see detail below.
  • Rate revision — cash patients only; three actions identified:
    • Flat 1% increase across all procedure/IP rates, rounded to nearest ₹10 — OP rates unchanged; +₹2.5 Cr upside assuming no volume drop.
    • Nursing cost increment (wage negotiation/strike) passed to patient bill — 40% increase in nursing charges; +₹7.5 Cr. *(To-do: benchmark IP room rates against competitors.)*
    • Top 50 procedures by value — contribution target of 58% to achieve 26% EBITDA; correction for under-contributing procedures: +₹2.5 Cr.
  • New BD hire in progress to build referral networks and institutional tie-ups; under-performing doctors to be replaced or incentivised — WIP (see below).
3. Lakeshore 2.0 — Brand Uplift
  • Organisation restructure: new org chart drafted with revised reporting lines — final version expected 20 March.
  • Website redesign underway; new site targeted to go live end of April. Preview design here → App design under consideration — Preview app here →
  • HR, Finance and Ops process and platform mapping underway to determine integration approach; identifying where systems and workflows need to be consolidated or replaced before implementation begins.
4. Compliance
  • Most material exposures: FY25 income tax return four months overdue (Sec 271B penalty risk up to ₹1.5 Cr); GSTR-9 annual return 72 days past deadline with daily penalties accruing; board minutes unsigned for three meetings, blocking DIR-12 and MGT-14 filings due in 22 days.

Other Notes
UNA Strike — Situation and Response
The United Nurses Association (UNA) strike has affected private hospitals across Kerala, including Lakeshore. Emergency and critical services at Lakeshore have remained fully operational throughout. At this point, we are not planning to take any significant further action — the situation is being monitored and management will respond if conditions materially change.
