VPS Lakeshore · Partner Commentary · Week of 16 March 2026
Structural momentum is building — but revenue activation and compliance cannot wait.
Three weeks into the programme, capex discipline is showing and cost identification is progressing. The harder work — activating revenue levers and resolving a governance backlog — now defines the programme's credibility.
Chairman's Office, VPS Lakeshore 16 March 2026 LHRC/BU/2026/002 · Strictly Confidential
The Programme
The Lakeshore 2.0 agenda is three weeks in. The programme has correctly identified its highest-value near-term levers — consumables rationalisation (₹40L immediate, ₹5 Cr structural), selective capex deferral, and a revenue enhancement programme anchored by tariff revision and new specialty launches. The FY27 capex budget has been set at a disciplined ₹82.2 Cr with ₹7 Cr of non-critical spend deferred. Capital is flowing to revenue-enabling projects, not capability aspirations — and that is the right instinct.
What is now critical is execution velocity. Two areas — revenue activation and compliance — are running materially below the pace required to deliver FY27 impact.
Where We Are
Cost rationalisation has identified the right targets but has not yet arrived at a contractable savings quantum. The ₹40L consumables opportunity must translate into signed vendor commitments within the fortnight. Compliance is a more serious concern: 27 of 62 items are already past due, including the FY25 income tax return, the GSTR-9 annual filing, and board minutes blocking two pending director filings. These cannot be managed through a tracker — they require a dedicated champion with a 60-day mandate and weekly board-level review.
The Call to Action
Three decisions this week will determine whether this programme delivers FY27 impact or defers it: commission a dedicated compliance task force with a single named officer and 60-day closure mandate; set 31 March as a non-negotiable deadline for tariff revision sign-off — revenue levers that slip to April become FY28 levers; and require a firm, contractable cost savings number from the rationalisation team before the next board update. The upside case of ₹575 Cr revenue and 25.8% EBITDA margin is still achievable — but only if Q1 is used, not observed.
