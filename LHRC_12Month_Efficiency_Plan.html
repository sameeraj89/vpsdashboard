💡 The 12-Month Thesis
LHRC is a debt-free ₹452 Cr hospital at 21.8% EBITDA — positioned at the inflection point of a landmark transformation. The 12-month plan runs in three distinct phases: Phase 1 (Apr–May) captures low-hanging fruit in staffing, rate revision, specialist onboarding and referral activation, while presenting the Board with a consolidated capex plan, Cancer Centre roadmap and HIS/ERP shortlist; Phase 2 (Jun–Sep) executes procurement rationalisation, material cost reform (COGS 28.5%→26.5%), North Kerala referral expansion, HIS/ERP kick-off, and utilities optimisation — Cancer Centre floor conversion is deferred to Phase 3 or later pending Board approval; Phase 3 (Oct–Mar) captures the full revenue upside via new specialist doctors, new specialties (IR-DSA, Cosmetology, Wellness, Geriatric and Palliative Care), international patient programme, HIS Phase 1 go-live and LINAC Upgrade AERB filing. The ₹50 Cr EBITDA uplift above budget is achievable by executing both the revenue engine (ARPOB ₹35K→₹41K, occupancy 53%→68%) and the cost lever (staffing ₹11 Cr + material costs ₹7 Cr + energy ₹1 Cr) simultaneously.

🎯 Phase 1 Mandate — Low-Hanging Fruit & Board Preparation
Phase 1 captures low-hanging fruit across revenue and cost within the first 60 days while preparing the Board with a full capex, cancer centre, and IT plan. Four immediate priorities: (1) Initiate staffing rationalisation in Nursing (630→569) and Pharmacy (93→50) — ₹11 Cr annualised opportunity; (2) Rate revision exercise across OP consultations, procedures and packages — no capital required, immediate ARPOB lever; (3) Onboard the first high-value specialist with OT allocation and referral pipeline ready from Day 1; (4) Activate referral channels through structured GP and specialist outreach with a dedicated liaison. The consolidated Board pack covering capex plan, Cancer Centre roadmap, LINAC Upgrade AERB timeline and HIS/ERP shortlist must be presented by end of Phase 1.

🔧 Phase 2 Mandate — Procurement, Material Costs & Revenue Ramp
Phase 2 builds on Phase 1's foundation with structural reform across procurement and revenue infrastructure. Vendor consolidation and the Material Cost Rationalisation programme target COGS from 28.5% to 26.5% of revenue — a ₹7 Cr combined opportunity. TPA renegotiation (top 5 insurers, 3 new agreements) grows insurance mix from 26% to 30%. The North Kerala and border district referral programme begins driving occupancy toward 68%. The HIS/ERP vendor is selected and implementation kicked off. Solar rooftop (500 KW) and HVAC IoT optimisation deliver ₹1 Cr energy savings. Cancer Centre floor-by-floor conversion (Main Block floors 6–8) is deferred to Phase 3 or later pending Board approval — this is a key revision from earlier planning.
