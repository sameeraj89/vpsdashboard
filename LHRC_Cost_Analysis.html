✓ Cost Efficiency from Volume Ramp
IP discharges +12.8% (21K→24.7K) and OP visits +17.3% spread fixed costs over higher throughput. Occupancy ramp to 62.5% reduces fixed cost per bed-day significantly — Employee + Power cost as % of revenue falls from 20.1% to 19.4%.
⚠ Cost Risk Factors
Doctor cost growing 44.7% vs revenue growth of 26.4% — ratio rises from 16.5% to 18.8%. Variable incentive pool (₹21.1 Cr) is volume-linked and will spike if discharges miss target. Capex at ₹150 Cr (+40% YoY) requires tight project execution to avoid overrun.

🔴 Gap: Doctor Cost %
At 18.8% of revenue (FY27), doctor cost is 300–400 bps above peers (14–16%). Driven by aggressive specialist hiring and 10% increment. If revenue targets are met, the fixed-cost leverage should bring this ratio down by FY28. Consider introducing tiered incentive structures to align cost with volume.
🟡 Watch: Total HR Cost %
HR at 38.5% of revenue is 500–800 bps above leading peers (Apollo ~33%, Fortis ~34%). LHRC's complex case mix (Transplants, Onco, Cath Lab) partially justifies the premium. A workforce productivity index (Revenue per FTE) tracking quarterly would help close the gap over 2–3 years.
🟢 Strength: Cost Leverage Driving Margin
Despite HR cost investment, fixed operating costs are growing slower than volume — producing the 390 bps EBITDA improvement (19.3% → 23.2%). This reflects cost discipline in Employee, Power, and R&M lines even as doctor investment scales up.
